Gameplay - Stats

There are several stats in the game that define the characteristics of a champion.
Health Point
- Total hit point of your champion, once this reache zero, you die and must wait until you respawn again in order to
play again
Health Regen
- How much health you regain automatically
Mana Point
- Total resource point you have. Resource can be mana or something else. You require resource to cast most spells.
Mana/Resource Regen
- How much mana/resource you regain automatically.
Attack Damage
- How much damage your auto attacks deal. Auto attacks are attacks your champion performs every interval of time without
spending any resource.
Magic Damage
- How much damage your spells deal. Some spells have magic scaling and so the higher your magic damage, the greater
damage your champion deals. The same can be applied for attack damage scaling spells.
Attack Penetration
- How much of damage resistance you ignore from your enemy.
Magic Penetration
- How much of magic damage resistance you ignore from your enemy.
Lethality
- Attack Penetration based on how strong your champion is.
Attack Speed
- How fast your champion performs auto attacks.
Tenacity
- How quickly your champion can remove itself from crowd controls. Crowd controls refers to an ability to cast
limitations to a champion's action. Stun for example, makes your champion unable to do anything during the duration.
Armor
- Attack damage resistance.
Magic Resist
- Magic damage resistance.
Range
- How far your spells/attacks reach.
Move Speed
- How fast your champion travels.
Life Steal
- How much health you regain from the percentage of damage you dealt when you auto attack.

Gameplay - Lanes and Roles

There are 5 different types of positions in League of Legends - Top, Mid, ADC, Sup, and Jungle. Players are not required to follow
this format of positions however, it has been deemed by many experimentation that one of each position is the best way to
make your team. Top lane is generally played by champions that can support other lanes and can stay in lane for a long time without
dying. In general, top laners can withstand a lot of damage before dying but doesn't deal much damage. Mid lane is generally
played by a champion that can cast spells from a distance or output high amount of damage from close range. Mid laners can usually
deal powerful bursts of damage. ADC refers to "attack damage carry". ADC are known as ADC because they are usually champions
that deal high amount of attack damage from a distance. Carry means to lead the game to victory. Supports are champions
that support mainly the ADC and the whole team in general. Supports vary in characteristics and stats greatly because most
champions are suited for playing the supporting role. Jungle refers to a champion that doesn't stay in any lane but stays in the
jungle most of the time. Junglers gank lanes when given the opportunity. Gank means to ambush an enemy.

Gameplay - Terminologies

There are several terminologies in League of Legends
1v1
- To challenge a player in 1 on 1 duel.
Ace
- When all five or three enemies are dead and are waiting for respawn time
Active
- An ability that requre activating.
AFK
- When a player leaves a game, they are AFK(away from keyboard). AFKs are punished with a ban.
Aggro
- Targeting priority of an A.I. or enemy champions.
AoE
- Area of effect.
Base
- The area containing the nexus, two towers guarding the nexus, and the fountain is considered the base.
Broken
- When something in the game is imbalanced.
Camp
- Where monsters in the jungle spawn.
Gank
- To ambush an enemy.
Drake
- Refers to the 5 different types of dragons that spawn in the dragon's nest. Dragon's nest is located between the bot and mid lane.
Farm
- To kill anything other than an enemy champion to gain gold.
Tank
- A champion that has built mostly health and damage resistance to survive as long as possible in a fight
Team fight
- An act of fighting a five against five.
GLHF
- Abbreviation for "good luck and have fun".
GG
- Abbreviation for "good game".

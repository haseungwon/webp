In League of Legends there are 3 different types of map in total however, in general most people prefer to play in the "Summoner's Rift"
and this website will be covering it. In the Summoner's Rift there are 3 main linear areas called "lane". Between these lanes is an
area referred to as "jungle". Every so often entities called "minions" will spawn in all 3 of the lanes and attack any enemy in sight
until they die. In the jungle, monsters will spawn after being slain and will stay passive until attacked. In each lane there are powerful
structures called "turrets" that will attack any enemy that approach it. Behind 3 turrets there lies a structure called the "inhibitor"
which when destroyed, cause a stronger minion to spawn in each lane. Behind the inhibitor are two turrets that guard the "nexus".
Once you destroy the nexus, the team that belongs to the destroyed nexus will lose the game. Use the slideshow above to
see what the quoted objects look like.

Basics of League of Legends - Shops and Champions

There are over 100 champions that you can choose to play as before the game begins. Each champions have different characteristics
and sets of skills that can be learned as you level up to become stronger. Champions each have different play styles but players
are not confined to playing a champion by their intended role. Players can choose which traits of a champion they will enhance by 
choosing which items to buy. Items can be bought by moving to the "fountain" an area just behind the nexus. In the shop interface
players can choose which items they would like to buy as long as they have enough in-game currency. Each items increase different
stats of a champion. Items can be bought by using an  in-game currency called gold. Gold is generated over time and you get a boost of it
everytime a structure is destroyed or an enemy is killed.

Each champion has 4 unique sets of skills and one unique passive. Passive is an ability that is constantly active with no need for 
the player to command the champion to use it. One of the 4 skills is called the ultimate. An ultimate skill is the most powerful
skill you can use and is on a long cooltime. Cooltime is the time it takes before you can reuse an ability after using it.
You as the summoner may choose up to two different summoner spells to use for any champions. Summoner spells are not
unique to champions and in general champions may choose up to two spells however, there is an exception. Some champions
may use several spells. Masteries is a trait, passive, and active ability you can choose for your champion before the game starts. Likewise summoner spells,
masteries are not unique traits and any champion may use up one main mastery, 5 sub masteries, and 3 stat masteries.
